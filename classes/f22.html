CS 8395: Selected Topics in Deep Learning

Deep learning has become an inseparable part of many tasks in AI/ML, ranging from language understanding and speech and image recognition to machine translation, planning, game playing, and autonomous driving, among many others. Expertise in deep learning is in high demand both in academic and industrial settings. This course provides a tour of a subset of representation learning topics in deep learning: auto-encoders from both Bayesian and Rate-Distortion (Information Bottleneck) perspectives, GANs, Flow models, Contrastive methods, and Score models. The course will provide brief historical context on the theory and guiding intuitions for each topic, as well as contemporary examples in the literature. In order to develop practical knowledge of the methods, students will complete a term project on one of the topics with guidance from the instructor.

Familiarity with multivariate calculus, linear algebra, probability theory, {TF,pytorch} suggested. Prerequisite CS 5262 or equivalent.
